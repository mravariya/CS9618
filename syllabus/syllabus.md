# Cambridge International AS & A Level Computer Science 9618 — Syllabus overview

This document summarises the **official syllabus content** for **Cambridge International AS & A Level Computer Science (9618)**. It is aligned with the **2027, 2028 and 2029** examination series syllabus (version 2, December 2025) and remains broadly consistent with the **2021 onwards** specification structure.

## Qualification aims

The syllabus aims to enable students to develop:

- **Computational thinking** skills  
- Understanding of **main principles** of solving problems using computers  
- Understanding of **computer systems** (software, data, hardware, communication, people)  
- Understanding of **communication methods**, networks, and the **internet**  
- Skills to **apply** this understanding to **computer-based solutions**

## Key concepts (syllabus)

| Concept | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| Computational thinking | Abstraction, decomposition, algorithmic thinking; solutions may use several technologies and languages. |
| Programming paradigms | Styles of approaching problems; choose appropriately when designing programs. |
| Communication | Rules and methods for moving data within a system or across the internet. |
| Computer architecture and hardware | How components and data are organised; how hardware and software work together. |
| Data representation and structures | Binary interpretations; structuring data for efficient access and transfer. |

## Assessment overview

**Calculators are not allowed** in any paper.

| Paper | Title | Duration | Marks | Content assessed |
| --- | --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| **1** | Theory fundamentals | 1h 30m | 75 | Sections **1–8** |
| **2** | Fundamental problem-solving and programming | 2h | 75 | Sections **9–12** (pseudocode; **no** live programming language in the exam) |
| **3** | Advanced theory | 1h 30m | 75 | Sections **13–20** |
| **4** | Practical | 2h 30m | 75 | Sections **19–20** (excluding low-level and declarative); **Java (console), VB .NET (console), or Python (console)** |

### Weightings

- **AS Level:** Paper 1 (50%) + Paper 2 (50%).  
- **A Level:** Each of Papers 1–4 contributes **25%**.

### Assessment routes

1. **AS only** — Papers 1 and 2 in the same series.  
2. **A Level staged** — AS in year 1 (Papers 1–2), then complete A Level (Papers 1–4 per carry-forward rules).  
3. **A Level linear** — Papers 1–4 in one series.

## Assessment objectives (summary)

Candidates are expected to demonstrate:

- **AO1** Knowledge and understanding of principles and concepts.  
- **AO2** Application of knowledge to design, program, analyse, and evaluate in familiar and **unfamiliar** contexts.

(Command words such as *state*, *describe*, *explain*, *justify* appear in the syllabus’s assessment details.)

## Teacher guidance (high level)

- Computer science is **practical**; centres need adequate hardware and **console-mode** HLL experience for Paper 4.  
- **Direct-access** file capability and **hard copy** are required for practical work.  
- Specimen materials and administration details for **Paper 4** are on the School Support Hub and Cambridge samples database.
