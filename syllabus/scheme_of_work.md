# Scheme of Work cross-reference (9618)

This page summarises the **Cambridge International Scheme of Work** for **Computer Science 9618** (for examination from **2021**), aligned with the syllabus structure used in this repository. Full detail, teaching activities, and formative assessment ideas are in the official PDF:

- `resources/references/originals/9618 Scheme of Work  (for examination from 2021).pdf`

Always treat the **syllabus** as the authority for assessment; the Scheme of Work is **guidance** for pacing and pedagogy.

## Introduction (summary)

- Supports lesson planning and **full syllabus coverage**; centres adapt pace and activities to their learners.
- Marks opportunities for **independent study (I)**, **formative assessment (F)**, and **extension** work.
- Emphasises **practical computer science** throughout the course.

## Key concepts (KC1–KC5)

| Tag | Concept | Role in the course |
| --- | --- | --- |
| KC1 | Computational thinking | Abstraction, decomposition, algorithmic thinking across problems and technologies. |
| KC2 | Programming paradigms | Choosing appropriate styles (e.g. procedural, OOP) for the task. |
| KC3 | Communication | Data transfer within systems and across networks/internet. |
| KC4 | Computer architecture and hardware | How components and rules enable functioning systems. |
| KC5 | Data representation and structures | Binary interpretations; organising data for access and transfer. |

## Guided learning hours (qualification level)

- **Cambridge International AS Level** — about **180** guided learning hours.  
- **Cambridge International A Level** — about **360** guided learning hours.  

Local timetabling and prior experience will change actual time needed.
